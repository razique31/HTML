S = vᵢt + ½at²: The Equation of Linear Motion

The formula S = vᵢt + ½at² describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration. "An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force." This famous quote emphasizes the role of forces in motion. This equation was originally written in 1905.

  • S represents the displacement,
  • vᵢ is the initial velocity,
  • t is the time,
  • a is the acceleration, and
  • ½at² is the additional displacement due to acceleration.
